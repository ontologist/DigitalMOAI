## TARGET POPULATION: CARE LEAVERS

**Who They Are:**
Youth who "age out" of foster care or leave orphanages/children's homes at age 18, forced into independence without family support networks.

**Why They're Vulnerable:**

### Japan-Specific Challenges:
- **Forced independence at 18**: Must leave orphanages immediately (legal age changed to 18 in 2022) [Civil Code Amendment, 2022]
- **Educational barriers**: Only 10% attend college (vs 36.1% nationally) [HRW, 2014]; only 73% complete high school (vs 81.5%) [HRW, 2014]
- **Guarantor crisis**: Cannot rent apartments or get jobs without guarantor (family member/sponsor) [HRW, 2014]
- **Life skills gap**: Many lack basic independent living skills [HRW, 2014]
- **Economic precarity**: Often end up in dead-end jobs, homeless, or in "night work" (sex work) [HRW, 2014]
- **Mental health**: 25% have disabilities [HRW, 2014]; high rates of trauma and PTSD
- **Social isolation**: No family connections for emotional/practical support

### Global Context (for comparison):
- **20% instantly homeless** upon aging out [NFYI, 2012-2015]
- **31-46% experience homelessness** by age 26 [Cutuli et al., 2016]
- **90% have experienced trauma** [NFYI, 2012-2015]
- **41% have mental health diagnosis**, 25% have PTSD [NFYI, 2012-2015]
- **Less than 3% earn college degree** [NFYI, 2012-2015]
- **Only 50% employed** by age 24 [NFYI, 2012-2015]
- **25% involved with criminal justice system** within 2 years [NFYI, 2012-2015]
