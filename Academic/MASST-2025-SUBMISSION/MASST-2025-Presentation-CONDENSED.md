### **Anticipated Questions & Answers**

**Q: "How do you handle consensus when MOAI members disagree?"**
A: Three-tier decision system: (1) Personal AI settings = individual autonomy, (2) Routine tasks = majority vote, (3) Critical decisions = unanimous or escalate to human discussion. AI suggests, humans decide.

**Q: "What if vulnerable person doesn't have 4 others to form MOAI?"**
A: Matchmaking system (in development) connects individuals with shared needs/interests while preserving privacy. Alternative: join existing MOAI with open spot (rare, as MOAIs tend to be stable).

**Q: "Privacy sounds great, but what about backups? Data loss?"**
A: Encrypted peer-to-peer replication across MOAI members' devices. Each member holds encrypted copy of group data. No single point of failure. IPFS integration for larger media files.

**Q: "Cultural adaptation beyond Japan?"**
A: Semantic ontology designed for cultural extensibility. Working with anthropologists to map community support structures globally (Korean "kye", African "susu", Caribbean "sou-sou"). Core principle: preserve local structures, add AI augmentation.

**Q: "Performance seems too good (0.16ms). What's the catch?"**
A: Local-first architecture means no network latency for core operations. 0.16ms is local semantic query time. P2P sync across MOAI members adds latency (typically <100ms on LAN), but non-blocking for user operations.

**Q: "How do you prevent AI from becoming 'too good' and replacing human judgment?"**
A: Design constraint: Default automation level is "suggestion only." Users must explicitly opt into automation. Group consensus required to change policies. Regular human meetings remain central to MOAI structure.

**Q: "Verification of safety claims?"**
A: Currently empirical verification through testing (2M+ operations) combined with design constraints from traditional MOAI structure (5-person limit, collective governance, human-in-the-loop for critical decisions). Seeking collaboration on formal verification methods for AI embedded in social structures. Open research question for MASST community!

**Q: "Scalability concerns - can this work beyond 5-person groups?"**
A: Intentionally doesn't scale past 5. Instead: proliferate MOAIs. Network effects through inter-MOAI coordination (planned), but core units remain small for trust and privacy.
