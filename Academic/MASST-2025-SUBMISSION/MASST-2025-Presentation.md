## Slide 7: Multi-Agent Architecture

**Traditional MOAI Structure (Preserved)**
- 5 human members who meet regularly
- Collectively govern automation policies
- 5-person limit prevents surveillance-scale data collection

**AI Agent Roles:**

**Personal AI Assistants** (1 per member)
- Learn individual preferences, accessibility needs, boundaries
- User-controlled automation levels

**Group Coordinator Agent**
- Scheduling with accessibility requirements
- Pattern monitoring with human verification
- Supports (never replaces) collective decisions

**Accessibility Accommodation Agent**
- Dynamic interface adaptation (visual, auditory, motor, cognitive)
- WCAG 2.1 AAA compliance
- Assistive technology integration

**Emergency Response Coordinator**
- Immediate group notification during crises
- Privacy-preserving location sharing (opt-in, time-limited)
- Human-confirmed emergency services connection

**Visual:** Network diagram showing 5 humans in center, each with personal AI assistant, surrounded by shared agents

## Slide 10: Trust Calibration - User Control

**Three User-Configurable Automation Levels**

**Level 1: Suggestion Only** (default)
- AI proposes actions with full transparency
- Waits for explicit human approval
- For new users and high-stakes decisions

**Level 2: Automatic with Notification**
- AI takes predefined actions
- Immediate notification + undo window (24hr default)
- For routine tasks

**Level 3: Automatic with Escalation**
- AI acts independently on routine tasks
- Immediately escalates anomalies/high-risk situations
- For experienced users with calibrated trust

**Group Consensus Required**
All 5 members collectively decide automation policies

**Visual:** Sliding scale showing three levels with use case examples

**Key Message:** *"Trust isn't binary‚Äîit's calibrated through experience and control"*
